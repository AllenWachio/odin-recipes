Beef Stew

A hearty and comforting dish, beef stew is a slow-cooked meal featuring tender chunks of beef simmered with vegetables like carrots, potatoes, and celery. The rich, savory broth is flavored with garlic, onion, tomato paste, and Worcestershire sauce, making it a perfect dish for chilly days.

Ingredients

  • 2 lbs beef chuck, cut into 1-inch cubes
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 1 large onion, chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 4 cups beef broth
  • 2 cups water
  • 3 large carrots, peeled and sliced
  • 2 potatoes, peeled and cubed
  • 2 celery stalks, sliced
  • 1 cup frozen peas
  • 2 tablespoons tomato paste
  • 1 tablespoon Worcestershire sauce
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • 1 bay leaf
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 2 tablespoons all-purpose flour (optional, for thickening)

Procedure

  1. Heat the oil in a large pot over medium-high heat. Add the beef cubes and brown on all sides. Remove beef and set aside.
  2. In the same pot, add onion and garlic. Cook until softened, about 5 minutes.
  3. Return beef to the pot. Stir in the tomato paste, then add beef broth, water, Worcestershire sauce, thyme, and bay leaf.
  4. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat to low. Cover and simmer for 1.5 hours.
  5. Add carrots, potatoes, and celery. Continue simmering for another 30 minutes, or until vegetables and beef are tender.
  6. If the stew needs thickening, mix flour with a small amount of cold water to form a slurry and stir into the stew. Simmer for a few more minutes.
  7. Add peas, season with salt and pepper, and cook for an additional 5 minutes.
  8. Remove bay leaf before serving.
